Clery Characteristics

Cleary's large conical strawberries weigh up to 40 g. This variety is suitable for both amateur and industrial cultivation. It can be grown both outdoors and in greenhouses.

Clery is a variety of early ripening, and the characteristic feature is the mass achievement of ripe berries. The whole period of collecting ripe fruits lasts 12-15 days. The average yield of the variety is 0.25-0.3 kg per bush or 290 kg / ha.


Plants are actively developing, in one season you can get 25-30 young rosettes from one bush of the mother, so there are no problems with the purchase of planting material. Strawberry blooms in early May, easily tolerates small frosts.

Container seedlings take root better because the roots are less damaged during planting The optimal planting date for Clery’s strawberries is considered to be early spring, right after the snow has melted. However, you can plant in the second half of August - mid-September. Before spring planting seedlings need to "harden" 3-4 days at a temperature of 10 ° C.

Planting Clery is designed for 4 years: during this period the variety retains its characteristics. The maximum harvest occurs in the 3rd year. Then productivity starts to drop and the berries are smaller.
