Joly Characteristics

It has medium early or medium late maturation (20-25 days later than honey and 3-5 days later than Clery). Great for amateur gardening and for small commercial cultivation.

It is characterized by large bush size and strength of the root system. Upright large leaves have a light green color and a medium wrinkled surface. Each bush forms a lot of long strong stems with light puberty, as well as a large number of whiskers.


The fragrant porridge of uniform reddish-pink color with all its juiciness and tenderness has sufficient density to withstand transport and storage better than many other commercial varieties. There are no gaps in the pulp during the heat or during the drought.

During fruiting, large smooth berries are poured on the bush Berries of large size and weight (20-35 g), one-dimensional. The largest fruits are formed at the beginning of the fertile period, and towards the end there is a slight fading of the berries. The bright, shiny, red skin is sprinkled with slightly indented tiny yellow seeds. During the period of technical maturity, the top of the berry is slightly colored, at full maturity the color unevenness disappears.
