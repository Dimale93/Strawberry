Clery Description

Many people love strawberries because of their pleasant taste and aroma. There are so many varieties of plants that you can enjoy fresh berries from late May to autumn by combining varieties with different ripening dates in your garden. And you can start this feast with Clery’s early strawberry that brings unusually sweet fruit.

Strawberry Clery appeared thanks to the efforts of Italian growers in 1996. Clery’s “parents” are Sweet Charlie and Onebor, and the place of origin is the Mazzoni Group (Comachio). The selection was made in 1998, the variety was tested under the designation A20-17.


The fruits are one-dimensional, large: the average weight is 30-40 g, in rare cases up to 50 g. The berries have the shape of a cone with a blunt thread. Ripe red fruits, in the phase of technical maturity - dark cherry. Cellulose is thick, without internal gaps, with a strong strawberry aroma, very sweet.

The Clery variety is characterized by a rather powerful bush and large berries Clery’s strawberries grow in tall and powerful shrubs. Large shiny leaves on long stalks are painted dark green. At the time of flowering on the bush are formed several times thick stems. Claire blooms lavishly, with large white flowers with a bright yellow center, the height of the inflorescence does not exceed the height of the leaves.
